Contributor License Agreement

Draft, pending attorney review. A CLA is a legal contract. This text is published so contributors can read it before they sign; it will be finalized by counsel. If you are about to contribute something substantial, say so in your first issue and we will confirm the current status.

Why this exists

VistA Forge is dual-licensed: freely under the AGPL-3.0-or-later, and commercially under a separate paid license (see Licensing). Offering the commercial license requires that Rafael Richards hold or control all rights in the codebase. If an outside contribution were AGPL-only, the combined work could no longer be licensed commercially — permanently, and for everyone.

This agreement preserves that ability. It does not take away your rights to your own contribution — you keep them (§2).

Scope — which repositories

This CLA applies to every AGPL-3.0-or-later repository in the org (the m-* and v-* toolchain, f-stdlib, and the grammar chain). It does not apply to the MIT extensions (vista-atlas, vista-compass), the Apache-2.0 toolkit and infrastructure repos, or m-modern-corpus — for those, inbound = outbound is sufficient.

How to accept

Acceptance is a signed entry committed to the repository — no third-party CLA service is used, and nothing you sign leaves the git history.

In the same change as your first contribution, add your block to CONTRIBUTORS.md in the repository you are contributing to: 

Name:                    Ada Lovelace
Contact:                 ada@example.org
Entity (if applicable):  —
Date:                    2026-07-22
I accept the vista-forge CLA (https://vista-forge.github.io/cla.html): yes

Entities: if you are contributing on behalf of an employer, an authorized signer must accept on the entity's behalf, by the same mechanism, naming the entity.
