Dual-licensed: AGPL-3.0, or commercial.

The VistA Forge toolchain is dual-licensed. You may use it under either the GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 (or, at your option, any later version), or a commercial license.

(a) The AGPL-3.0-or-later option

Free, and the default. The full license text ships in the LICENSE file of every repository. In practice, for the command-line tools (m fmt, m lint, m lsp, m test, v pkg, …) the AGPL behaves like the ordinary GPL: its distinguishing clause, §13, only applies when someone modifies the program and offers it to users over a network. A CLI does neither.

§13 is genuinely load-bearing for v-web and STDHTTPD, which are network services. If you modify those and serve them to users, the AGPL requires you to offer those users the corresponding source.

(b) The commercial option

A commercial license is available for use in proprietary or closed products, hosted services, or environments — including most U.S. federal systems and their integrators — that cannot accept the AGPL's network source-disclosure obligation.

To enquire about a commercial license, open an issue titled “Commercial license enquiry” on any public VistA Forge repository at github.com/vista-forge, and a maintainer will follow up. Please do not include confidential terms in a public issue — the first reply will move the conversation to a private channel.

Contributing

Because the toolchain is dual-licensed, outside contributions require a Contributor License Agreement. Offering a commercial license requires that the maintainer hold or control all rights in the codebase; without a CLA, an AGPL-only contribution would foreclose that permanently. The CLA does not take away your rights to your own contribution — you keep them.
